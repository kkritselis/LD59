CAPTAIN'S LOG — SOL DATE 2847.6

Expedition Vessel: Arden IV

We were three days out from the relay beacon when something hit us. Something deliberate — a pulse that fried every navigation system on the ship simultaneously.

We went down hard.

I am the only one who walked away from the wreck. The hangar module survived the crash mostly intact, which means I have shelter, a fighter, and whatever equipment I can salvage from the surrounding terrain. I have sent a distress beacon on every frequency I can generate. So far, nothing has answered. I need to build a signal tower and send a distress call to the fleet.

Whatever lives on this world has been watching since we landed. I can see them on the ridge lines at dusk — shapes that don't move like animals. Last night they got closer. Soon, I expect they'll be closer still.

I don't know how long rescue will take. I don't know how many of them there are. What I know is this: the hangar holds, or nothing else matters. I will push out as far as I can, take everything this planet will give me, and I will build something they cannot break.

— Commander Reyes, Arden IV
